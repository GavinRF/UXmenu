Design Process Stages

Select tools from each stage of the UX design process to build a custom UX toolkit*.


In practice the four design stages do not follow any specific order, can occur in parallel, and should be repeated iteratively. As such, each stage should be seen as a different mode that contributes to a project, rather than sequential steps.

Discovery
0

A discovery is a preliminary phase in the UX-design process that involves researching the problem space, framing the problem(s) to be solved, and gathering enough evidence and initial direction on what to do next. Discoveries do not involve testing hypotheses or solutions

Focus Groups

Moderated discussion to obtain users’ experiences and expectations with a product or process. Get feedback from group of users to inform the next release.

required work
add_circle

During COVID or with low budgets, UX designers may use zoom sessions or similar software to conduct focus groups. 5-10 users to meet in person to discuss thoughts, opinions, and behaviors

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Notes - Qualitative Data

  • build_circleTools

    Access to users, focus group facilitators, minimum of 4-6 people recommended for focus group. If virtual participants should have access to computer camera, microphone & video conferencing software.

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    1 – 2 hrs/session

additional
add_circle

Assumptions

A wireframe, product, or framework exists to get user feedback on.

Recommended Pairings

surveys, affinity diagraming contextual inquiry, interviews, persona’s, card sorting, usability testing.

Use When

You want to start a discussion with your user group.

User Recruitment

Recruit participants who are willing and able to provide feedback in for activities outlined in HCD/UX plan. These could be current or prospective users.

required work
add_circle

Obtain a pool of representative to contact for HCD activities

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    User recruitment request document, user recruitment screener, participant contact lists

  • build_circleTools

    Email, Spreadsheet tool, Forms/Questionnaire tool

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    From Internal Stakeholders: 1-2 hours per research method From UX Team: 2-3 hours per research method

additional
add_circle

Resources Involved

SMEs, Business Owners, Decision Makers, Incentives

Disclaimers

  • This may require discussions about recruitment needs, approvals, and/or recruitment screeners
  • User recruitment approach heavily depends on any user communication constraints on each project

Surveys

Collect measurable information from a sample of users using a set of written questions. To gather feedback on a specific topic, often measurable feedback.

required work
add_circle

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Quantitative and/or qualitative data analysis, sometimes KPI’s

  • build_circleTools

    Word processing or survey tool (SurveyMonkey, Microsoft Forms, Google Forms)

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    2 hrs to create, 2 hrs analysis

additional
add_circle

Client Stakeholders Time Commitment

5-20 min to complete by users. At least 1 hour with client to help shape the questions in the surveys if needed. A UX survey blueprint is a helpful tool to help formulate survey questions.

Contextual Inquiry

Observe users in their natural work environment, ask clarifying questions about their processes and tools

required work
add_circle

Gather data on user needs directly from users and observation

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Qualitative data analysis

  • build_circleTools

    Notetaking tool, recording (audio and/or video), screensharing tools, web camera

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    From Internal Stakeholders: 1-2 hrs/session From UX Team: 3-4 hrs/session – preparing for session, conducting session, synthesizing and analyzing findings

additional
add_circle

Disclaimers

  • We recommend including multiple facilitators in each session for observation and note-taking
  • Can be in person or remote. If remote, participants should share their screen and, if possible, turn on their web cameras for full observation

Design Evaluation

Examine a product and provide recommendations to make sure it meets design best practices, project-specific user needs and objectives. May include informal feedback methods, redlining, heuristic evaluations, usability testing and more.

required work
add_circle

Pinpoint potential issues and make recommendations based on project-specific user needs, objectives, and design best practices.

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Document. Potentially red-lined / annotated screenshots.

  • build_circleTools

    Any tool that can annotate images or document recommendations

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    From Internal Stakeholders: 1-2 hrs/session From UX Team: 1-2 hrs/session

additional
add_circle

Disclaimers

  • May take multiple sessions, depending on the scope

Usability Testing

Use prototype, work-in-progress version, or current version of a product to evaluate how well a representative user can complete typical tasks

required work
add_circle

Identify usability problems, collect data on how the product performs in relation to user needs and satisfaction. Find opportunities for usability improvements

  • upload_fileDeliverables

    Notes (qualitive and quantitative data), design iterations, user stories

  • build_circleTools

    Prototype or product, screen recording software/mic for remote testing or someone to record in-person sessions

  • scheduleTime Commitment

    From Internal Stakeholders: 1 hr/session From UX Team: 4-5 hrs/session to prepare, conduct, and synthesize findings

additional
add_circle

Un-moderated

Moderated

Audit a subset of content across sections

In-person

Remote

Users and moderator communicate via internet video or call software such as Skype, Teams, Zoom etc. (Note: this is especially important during the COVID-19 Pandemic)

Disclaimers

  • UAT ≠ Usability Testing
  • Various techniques can be used including 5 second testing, think-aloud protocols, eye tracking testing. etc
  • Recruit users that have not been trained on the current version or prototype
  • Iterative and should occur throughout the entire product cycle
